Why Speed is better than Speed 2

When it comes to action movies, few can compare to the excitement and adrenaline rush provided by the original 1994 film "Speed." Starring Keanu Reeves and Sandra Bullock, the movie follows the story of a bomb on a bus that will explode if the vehicle slows down below 50 miles per hour. The film was a smash hit and became an instant classic, spawning a sequel in 1997 called "Speed 2: Cruise Control." While the sequel had its moments, it was nowhere near as good as the original. Here are just a few reasons why "Speed" is better than "Speed 2."


The premise is stronger

The original "Speed" had a simple, yet effective premise that kept audiences on the edge of their seats: if the bus slows down, everyone dies. The stakes were high, and the tension was palpable. In contrast, "Speed 2" took place on a cruise ship, and while the threat was still there (a computer virus takes over the ship), it didn't have the same sense of urgency as the first film.


Keanu Reeves

Keanu Reeves was the heart and soul of "Speed." His portrayal of the hero cop Jack Traven was both believable and charismatic. He brought a sense of gravitas to the role that made the audience root for him from start to finish. Unfortunately, Reeves did not return for the sequel, and his absence was sorely felt.


The villain

Dennis Hopper played the villain in "Speed," and he was brilliant. He was menacing and unpredictable, and his performance elevated the film to another level. In "Speed 2," Willem Dafoe played the bad guy, but his performance felt flat in comparison to Hopper's. The character was also less interesting, and his motivations were less clear.


The action sequences

While "Speed 2" had its fair share of action scenes, they were not as memorable as the ones in the original. The bus jump scene in "Speed" is one of the most iconic moments in action movie history, and the film had several other pulse-pounding moments. In contrast, "Speed 2" had a few good action scenes, but nothing that really stood out.


In conclusion, while "Speed 2" is not a terrible movie, it pales in comparison to the original. "Speed" had a stronger premise, a better cast, and more memorable action scenes. It remains a classic of the action movie genre and is well worth watching again and again.
