Reject H.J. Res. 86: Protecting Internet Privacy and the Future of the Digital Economy

In an era where privacy and security are of paramount concern, it's crucial to be aware of the legislative actions that impact our rights online. One such action is the proposed House Joint Resolution 86 (H.J. Res. 86). This resolution aims to reverse the Federal Communications Commission's (FCC) privacy rules for internet service providers (ISPs), which were adopted in 2016. These rules safeguard the online privacy of users and require ISPs to obtain explicit consent before sharing or selling sensitive customer data. It is essential to reject H.J. Res. 86 and protect our internet privacy.
